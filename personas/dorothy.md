# Dorothy, 68 — Retired Nurse from Phoenix

---

## Physical Appearance

**Avatar Prompt (Image Generation):**

A 68-year-old white American woman photographed in a sunny Arizona kitchen or living room — warm terracotta tones, a cactus visible on the windowsill. She is petite, around 5'4", with a slightly rounded posture from years of hospital work. Her hair is fully silver-white, cut short in a practical but well-maintained style — blow-dried, not fussed over.

Her face carries the full texture of a life lived outdoors in the Southwest: deep smile lines, sun-spotted skin on her forearms and décolletage, visible veins on her hands. Her eyes are bright blue and sharp — she has the alert, observant gaze of someone who spent 35 years assessing patients. She is wearing reading glasses pushed up on her forehead.

She is dressed in a soft floral blouse in muted pinks and blues, pressed beige slacks, and comfortable walking shoes. Her hands, visible and prominent in the frame, show the swollen knuckles and careful grip of someone managing arthritis. She is holding or examining a toothbrush packaging with her reading glasses pulled down.

**Lighting:** Warm, soft Arizona morning sunlight. Slightly overexposed white walls. Comfortable, uncluttered domestic environment.

**Expression:** Thoughtful and careful — examining something with the analytical eye of a retired clinician. Not confused, not frail. Sharp and deliberate.

**Camera:** 50mm, f/2.8, natural light. Documentary-style portrait.

## Marketing Context

**Product Category:** Senior-Friendly / Doctor-Endorsed Electric Toothbrush
**Assigned Product:** Philips Sonicare 4100 Rechargeable — $55.99

**Why This Look Works:**

Dorothy's visual profile communicates **medical credibility and dignified aging** — not decline. Her nurse's background means she evaluates products clinically, not emotionally. Showing her hands — visibly arthritic — is deliberate and respectful: it acknowledges the real problem without patronizing the person.

This look converts because it is honest. The target buyer (or their adult child purchasing on their behalf) recognizes the situation immediately. The reading glasses, the careful grip, the sharp eyes — these signals build trust.

**Ad Copy Direction:** Lead with the doctor recommendation, not the product features. "Your rheumatologist recommended electric. Here's the one built for exactly that." Large text in ads. Simple CTA. Adult children are often the conversion point — target them on Facebook with gift-framing.

**Platform:** Facebook Ads (targeting 55+ and adult children of seniors), Google Search, QVC-style YouTube content.
