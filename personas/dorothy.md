She is petite, around 5'4", with a slightly rounded posture from years of hospital work. Her hair is fully silver-white, cut short in a practical but well-maintained style — blow-dried, not fussed over.

The brush has a notably lightweight, non-intimidating design.

**Expression:** Thoughtful and careful — examining something with the analytical eye of a retired clinician. Not confused, not frail. Sharp and deliberate.

## Marketing Context

**Product Category:** Senior-Friendly / Doctor-Endorsed Electric Toothbrush
**Assigned Product:** Philips Sonicare 4100 Rechargeable — $55.99

**Why This Look Works:**

Dorothy's visual profile communicates **medical credibility and dignified aging** — not decline. Her nurse's background means she evaluates products clinically, not emotionally. Showing her hands — visibly arthritic — is deliberate and respectful: it acknowledges the real problem without patronizing the person.

This look converts because it is honest. The target buyer (or their adult child purchasing on their behalf) recognizes the situation immediately. The reading glasses, the careful grip, the sharp eyes — these signals build trust.

**Ad Copy Direction:** Lead with the doctor recommendation, not the product features. "Your rheumatologist recommended electric. Here's the one built for exactly that." Large text in ads. Simple CTA. Adult children are often the conversion point — target them on Facebook with gift-framing.

**Platform:** Facebook Ads (targeting 55+ and adult children of seniors), Google Search, QVC-style YouTube content.
